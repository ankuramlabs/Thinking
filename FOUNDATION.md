# 1. Preamble

Thinking is an open, implementation-independent standard for engineering, representing, organizing, reasoning about, and evolving knowledge.

This document establishes the constitutional foundation of the Thinking Standard.

All Architecture Decision Records (ADRs), Specifications (SPECs), Standards (STDs), Reference Materials (REFs), and implementations derive their authority from this document.

This document is intentionally stable and shall evolve only through explicit constitutional review.

---

# 2. Mission

The mission of the Thinking Standard is:

> **To establish a universal, implementation-independent standard for semantic knowledge engineering that enables humans and intelligent systems to create, preserve, exchange, reason about, and evolve knowledge consistently across time, domains, and technologies.**

---

# 3. Scope

The Thinking Standard defines:

* The philosophical foundation of the standard.
* The conceptual architecture for semantic knowledge engineering.
* The vocabulary and terminology used throughout the standard.
* The normative specifications governing knowledge representation.
* The architectural principles for implementations.
* The relationship between specifications and implementations.

The Thinking Standard does **not** define:

* A programming language.
* A database.
* A file format.
* A storage engine.
* A user interface.
* A software application.

These are implementation concerns.

---

# 4. Vision

The long-term vision of the Thinking Standard is to become a durable, open foundation for knowledge engineering, independent of programming languages, software platforms, artificial intelligence models, storage technologies, and organizational boundaries.

The standard seeks to enable interoperable implementations capable of preserving meaning across generations of technology.

---

# 5. Core Principles

The Thinking Standard is governed by the following constitutional principles.

## Principle 1 — Meaning Before Representation

Meaning is primary.

Representations exist only to preserve and communicate meaning.

Representations may change.

Meaning should remain stable.

---

## Principle 2 — Semantics Before Syntax

Syntax defines structure.

Semantics defines interpretation.

Implementations shall preserve semantics regardless of syntax.

---

## Principle 3 — Knowledge is Technology Independent

Knowledge shall remain independent of:

* Programming languages
* Databases
* File formats
* Version control systems
* Artificial intelligence models
* Communication protocols

Technology is replaceable.

Knowledge is not.

---

## Principle 4 — Explicit Structure

Knowledge shall be represented using explicit structures rather than implicit conventions.

Relationships shall be explicit.

Identity shall be explicit.

Metadata shall be explicit.

---

## Principle 5 — Evolution Without Loss

Knowledge may evolve.

Its history shall remain traceable.

Its provenance shall remain preserved.

---

## Principle 6 — Open Interoperability

The Thinking Standard shall remain vendor-neutral, implementation-neutral, and openly implementable.

No implementation defines the standard.

Implementations conform to the standard.

---

# 6. Constitutional Architecture

The Thinking Standard is organized into five architectural layers.

```
Foundation
        ↓
Architecture
        ↓
Specification
        ↓
Standards
        ↓
Reference
```

Each layer depends only upon the layers above it.

Lower layers shall not redefine higher-layer concepts.

---

# 7. Normative Hierarchy

The following precedence applies throughout the repository.

1. FOUNDATION
2. Architecture Decision Records (ADR)
3. Specifications (SPEC)
4. Standards (STD)
5. Reference Material (REF)
6. Implementations

Where conflicts exist, the higher-precedence artifact shall govern.

---

# 8. Governance

Constitutional artifacts evolve through deliberate review.

Changes to this document should be:

* Rare
* Explicit
* Backward-aware
* Architecturally justified

Subordinate artifacts may evolve more frequently provided they remain consistent with this foundation.

---

# 9. Stewardship

The Thinking Standard is intended to be maintained through transparent architectural governance.

Stewards are responsible for preserving the integrity, consistency, and long-term coherence of the standard.

Implementers are responsible for producing conforming implementations.

---

# 10. Long-Term Commitment

The Thinking Standard is designed with the expectation that technologies will evolve while the underlying principles remain durable.

The standard therefore prioritizes:

* conceptual stability,
* semantic consistency,
* implementation independence,
* interoperability,
* and long-term maintainability.

---

# 11. Amendment Process

Amendments to this document shall be considered constitutional changes.

Constitutional amendments should be introduced only when existing principles are demonstrably insufficient or internally inconsistent.

Architectural convenience alone is not sufficient justification for constitutional change.

---

# 12. Conformance

A repository, implementation, or derivative specification claiming conformance to the Thinking Standard shall not contradict the constitutional principles defined in this document.

Conformance to this Foundation is a prerequisite for conformance to all subsequent specifications.

---

# 13. Relationship to Other Artifacts

This document serves as the constitutional root of the Thinking Standard.

It governs:

* Foundation documents
* Architecture Decision Records (ADR)
* Specifications (SPEC)
* Standards (STD)
* Reference materials (REF)
* All conforming implementations

No subordinate artifact may redefine or contradict this document.

---

# 14. Closing Statement

The Thinking Standard exists to provide a durable, implementation-independent foundation for semantic knowledge engineering.

Its purpose is not to prescribe technology, but to establish the principles by which knowledge can be represented, reasoned about, preserved, and evolved consistently across changing implementations and future generations of intelligent systems.
